Personalization
That Writes Itself

Imagine two companies with similar standard data points:


  • Location: USA
  • Team Size: 11 - 50
  • Industry: IT Services
  • Keywords: SaaS, B2B
  • Trigger: Hiring an SDR


Typically, sales teams would base their outreach on these
basics. Some might scrape LinkedIn for shallow
personalization, referencing a prospect’s university or an
outdated post. But what if they had deeper insights? Adding
just a few unique data points can reveal critical context about
each company’s specific challenges and goals.



  • Example:


  • Company A and Company B are both hiring their first SDR,
    with founders managing all sales. However, they face vastly
    different challenges:


  • In Company A, the founder is a first-time entrepreneur with a
    technical background and no experience in outbound
    marketing. They’ll likely rely heavily on the SDR for both
    strategy and execution.


In Company B, it’s the founder’s second company, and they
previously worked as an Account Executive for four years. They
understand the outbound process but may lack the time to
oversee it fully.


While these companies appear similar on the surface, each
requires a tailored approach. The founder of Company A will
need guidance and support on outbound fundamentals, while
the founder of Company B may be looking for time-saving
solutions and effective delegation.


  • With these deeper data points - such as sales leadership
    presence, founder background, and outbound experience - you
    can create more relevant and personalized campaigns that
    address the real challenges of each prospect.
